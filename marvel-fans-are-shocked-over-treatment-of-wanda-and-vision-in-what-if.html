Marvel Fans Are Shocked Over Treatment Of Wanda And Vision In 'What If'

Spoilers for WandaVision and What If...? aheadIt looks like there really is no happy ending for Wanda and Vision, one of the most beloved couples in the entire Marvel Cinematic Universe.

After suffering with the characters played by Elizabeth Olsen and Paul Bettany on Disney+'s show WandaVision, animated series What If...? gave viewers some more heartache over the two superheroes' fate.

Created by A.C. Bradley, the animated series explores what would happen if major moments in the MCU films occurred differently. Vision appears on the series too, voiced by Bettany, while Wanda is voiced by a different actor than Olsen.

Marvel Fans Protest Treatment Of Wanda And Vision In 'What If...?'

In a recent episode, titled "What If... Zombies?!", Wanda Maximoff aka Scarlet Witch is infected with a virus that turns her into a zombie. Vision, by her side, is luring survivors to a camp in order to feed them to Wanda.

In this alternate story, too, Vision ends up perish. Fans couldn't handle the storyline, once again seeing the couple separate in a tragic manner, and took to Twitter to share their feelings.

"First Wanda brings Vision back to life in Westview and now Vision is trying to keep zombie Wanda alive and literally dies bc he can’t leave her or live without her god I hate it here!" one fan wrote.

"marvel is SICK for this parallel wanda and vision could never catch a break in any universe," another fan noted a parallel with Avengers: Infinity War.

"VISION STILL LOVES WANDA EVEN IF SHE’S A ZOMBIE. THE MF EVEN FEEDS HER," another fan wrote.

"Marvel really just made us watch Vision die for like, the fifth f*cking time. Y’all are evil," one person tweeted.

WandaVision Can Make MCU History At The Emmys

WandaVision has made MCU history by becoming the first Marvel Studios project to be nominated at the Emmys last July.

The series is the first Marvel project to be nominated for Outstanding Limited Or Anthology Series. A love letter to television packing an emotional punch, the Disney+ show created by Jac Shaeffer premiered in January this year to rave reviews.

The first series in Phase Four of the MCU, WandaVision has scored a total of 23 Emmy nominations, including nods to its stars in the major acting categories.

WandaVision is the third most-nominated series at the Emmys this year — only The Crown and The Mandalorian did better, scoring 24 nods each.

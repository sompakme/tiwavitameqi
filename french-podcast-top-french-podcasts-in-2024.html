French Today’s Monthly Audio Magazine

If you’re looking for an engaging and immersive way to learn French, look no further than French Today’s Monthly Audio Magazine. This innovative subscription offers a unique opportunity to learn the language through audio, while also exploring various aspects of French culture and tradition.

Each month, French Today provides subscribers with four new articles narrated by online teacher Camille. What sets this magazine apart is that each article is recorded twice – once in slow, enunciated French, and again in faster, conversational French as you would hear on the streets of France. This dual recording format allows learners to practice their comprehension skills at different speeds and gradually get accustomed to the rhythm and cadence of authentic French speech.

The articles featured in the Monthly Audio Magazine cover a wide range of fascinating topics, including French culture, stories, traditions, and notable places in France. Whether you’re interested in learning about the history of French cuisine, exploring famous landmarks like the Eiffel Tower, or delving into the works of renowned French authors, this magazine has something for everyone.

By subscribing to the monthly magazine, learners gain instant access to the extensive library of 36 articles recorded by Camille. This allows you to dive deep into French language and culture, building your vocabulary, improving your listening skills, and gaining a deeper understanding of French society.

French Today’s Monthly Audio Magazine provides an immersive and enjoyable way to learn French through audio lessons. Whether you’re a beginner or an advanced learner, this resource offers a valuable addition to your language learning journey.

Little Talk In Slow French

Looking for a relaxing way to practice your French conversation skills? Little Talk In Slow French is the perfect solution. This podcast features episodes with a native French speaker who discusses topics she is passionate about. The episodes are conducted entirely in French, allowing you to immerse yourself in the language. Don’t worry if you’re just starting out – English explanations are provided along the way to help you understand the content.

Each episode of Little Talk In Slow French is around 20-35 minutes long, giving you ample opportunity to practice your listening skills. Whether you’re a beginner or an intermediate learner, this podcast is a great resource to improve your French comprehension and expand your vocabulary.

Little Talk In Slow French is completely free to listen to, making it accessible to French learners of all budgets. Take advantage of this opportunity to listen to a native French speaker and enhance your language skills.

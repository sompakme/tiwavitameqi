Alton Mason – The First Black Male Model for Chanel

Alton Mason made history in the fashion industry when he became the first Black male model to walk for Chanel. Karl Lagerfeld, the late creative director of Chanel, personally selected Mason to be part of the 2018 Métier d’Art collection. This significant milestone in Chanel’s century-long history propelled Mason to stardom and cemented his status as a top model.

“Being the first Black male model for Chanel was a dream come true. It was a historic moment that celebrated diversity and inclusion in the fashion industry. Karl Lagerfeld’s vision and acknowledgment created a significant impact, not just for me but for future generations of Black models,”

Even after Lagerfeld’s passing, his endorsement continued to shape Mason’s career, opening doors to more opportunities and collaborations. Mason’s presence as the first Black male model for Chanel represents a breakthrough, marking a step towards greater diversity and inclusion in the fashion world.

Throughout his career, Alton Mason has defied industry norms and broken barriers, paving the way for a more inclusive and representative fashion industry. His achievements as a Black male model for Chanel elevate him to an influential position within the fashion world.

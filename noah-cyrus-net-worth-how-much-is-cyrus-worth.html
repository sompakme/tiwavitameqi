Rakim’s Early Life and Career

Rakim, born William Michael Griffin Jr., was raised in Wyandanch, New York, and developed an early interest in rap. His passion for music led him to form the influential duo Eric B & Rakim in the mid-1980s, alongside DJ Eric B. Together, they would go on to revolutionize the rap industry and leave an indelible mark on the genre.

With his unique lyrical style and smooth delivery, Rakim quickly gained attention and acclaim as a rap artist. Known for his intricate rhyme schemes and introspective lyrics, he showcased a level of artistry and sophistication that set him apart from his contemporaries. The duo released several successful albums, including the critically acclaimed “Paid in Full” and “Follow the Leader,” which solidified Rakim’s place as one of the greatest MCs in rap history.

Rakim’s rap career not only defied expectations but also influenced generations of artists to come. His innovative approach to storytelling and his ability to craft intricate verses inspired the likes of Jay-Z, Nas, and Eminem, among many others. Rakim’s impact on the rap industry continues to be felt today, as his legacy serves as a foundation for the evolution of the genre.

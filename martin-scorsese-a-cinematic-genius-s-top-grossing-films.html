Martin Scorsese: A Cinematic Genius's Top Grossing Films

Highlights

  • Martin Scorsese's new film "Killers of the Flower Moon" is a thrilling Western crime drama based on a true story, set in the 1920s and featuring an all-star cast.
  • Scorsese's top-grossing films include "Goodfellas," "The Color Of Money," "Casino," and "Cape Fear," each earning impressive worldwide box office numbers.
  • Scorsese's career spans over five decades, during which he has won numerous awards, collaborated with renowned actors, and drawn inspiration from classic films, making him a true master of storytelling.

Martin Scorsese, a name known for pure brilliance in the world of cinema, has been masterfully creating unforgettable films for over five decades. Not only has he gained immense critical acclaim, but his creations have also struck box office gold.

Join us as we go deep into the fascinating realm of Martin Scorsese's new film, and explore some of his highest-grossing masterpieces. We'll also take a closer look at his personal life, numerous accolades, collaborations with esteemed actors, educational background, and influential sources.

New Film: Killers Of The Flower Moon - A Wild Ride Into History

Hold onto your seats, because Martin Scorsese delivers yet another heart-stopping thrill ride with his highly anticipated film Killers of the Flower Moon. This monumental Western crime drama takes inspiration from David Grann's gripping 2017 book bearing the same title.

Prepare to be transported back in time as you venture through this labyrinthine tale set during the roaring 1920s within Osage Nation. It all begins when oil is discovered on tribal land – a discovery that sets off a chain reaction leading to an astounding series of murders that shock both locals and outsiders alike.

This mind-blowing tale is about to burst onto the cinema scene, and unsurprisingly for Scorsese, he's rounded up a dream team cast. We're talking old school leading man Leonardo DiCaprio alongside the fabulous duo of Robert De Niro and Lily Gladstone.

The excitement for this grand spectacle has climbed to its peak, as it made its long awaited debut at the notoriously prestigious 76th Cannes Film Festival on May 20, 2023, and is set to be released in theaters in the United States on October 20, 2023. Now, let's look at some of the biggest movies that have ever graced our screens by Martin Scorsese – and when we say big, we mean these films are legends in their own right.

We're diving into the world of Martin Scorsese, a director who knows how to whip up an unforgettable story like no other.

So grab your popcorn and get ready for a journey through his top grossing masterpieces.

Goodfellas (1990)

First up on our list is Goodfellas, a mobster bonanza that never fails to impress viewers, first time and repeat. This iconic flick has earned its place at number one with a worldwide haul of $46,879,633!

Talk about making it rain; this film did just that.

The Color Of Money (1986)

Next on the list, we've got Paul Newman and Tom Cruise in The Color Of Money. It might be all about pool hustling, but don't let that fool you – this movie means business at the box office, with a staggering global gross total of $52,293,982 under its belt.

Casino (1995)

Get ready to roll the dice and head into the world of casinos with Casino. Here's another Scorsese masterpiece starring three powerhouse actors – Joe Pesci, Robert De Niro, Sharon Stone, and Martin Scorsese himself in the director's chair! These legends banded together for this reunion flick — and it raked in over $116,112,375 from all corners of the globe.

Cape Fear (1991)

Last but certainly not least is Cape Fear, a wickedly thrilling remake that sent chills down our spines back in 1991. Brace yourselves, because this nail-biting film pulled off an astonishing $182,291,969 worldwide! Now that'll make your jaw drop.

What You Didn't Know About Martin Scorsese

Now, we're diving deeper into the world of Martin Scorsese. Sure, he's the maestro behind those top-grossing films we all know and love, but his career is much more than that.

This cinematic legend has been delivering classic after classic for more than five decades now, and let me tell you, he's still eager to wow us with his storytelling prowess. Despite being vocal about his distaste for today's flashy blockbusters in Hollywood, Scorsese has managed to build such a massive fanbase that even his latest flicks are lighting up the box office.

The Man Behind The Camera

Martin Scorsese was born in New York City on November 17th in 1942, and coming from humble beginnings didn't stop this industry legend from reaching great heights. Scorsese's love life has been colorful, as he had not one or two but five marriages; at the moment, Helen Schermerhorn Morris owns the title of Mrs. Scorsese.

And speaking of family matters, Scorsese is father to three children – including one named for her mother (Julia Cameron), Domenica Cameron-Scorsese. Scorsese's family history and life experiences are reflected in many of his most notable works, many of which center on the Italian-American experience.

Awards And Accolades

If there's one thing that Martin Scorsese knows how to do, it's bringing home awards at a staggering pace – this Hollywood heavyweight has more trophies than you can count on both hands. Scorsese bagged an Academy Award (also known as an Oscar) for Best Picture in 2007, acknowledging his film The Departed.

That's not counting the prestigious British BAFTA Awards - Scorsese won four of those for his work. On top of that, he has not one, not two, but three Emmy Awards to his lengthy credit – it seems that every time Scorsese attends an award ceremony, he waltzes out with his arms bulging under the weight of a veritable army of shiny golden statues.

Collaborations With Actors

Scorsese sure knows how to round up a stellar cast – and his diverse crew of collaborators are definitely skilled at making sparks fly on screen. Whether it's Frank Vincent delivering charming monologues or Harry Northup stealing scenes left and right – Scorsese's carefully hand-picked team never ceases to amaze!.

Scorsese's collaborations are legendary, and they've gifted us some of the most incredible performances in the history of film.

Education

You might be surprised at how his education played an important role in shaping Scorsese's cinematic genius. Going all the way back to 1968, Scorsese earned an MA from New York University's Steinhardt School of Culture, Education, and Human Development.

It was there that he honed his creative skills and gained deep insights into the world around him. But it doesn't stop there – Scorsese continued his pursuit for knowledge by obtaining degrees in film communications from none other than New York University’s prestigious School of Film; the flimmaker wanted to equip himself with all the necessary tools to create extraordinary movies that would mesmerize audiences worldwide.

Influences

Now let's look at what shaped Scorsese as an artist - his influences – Scorsese understands that art evolves through inspiration from those who walked before us, or in other words, his own favorite flicks.

Being well-versed in cinema history is essential for Scorsese; it helps him re-imagine classic films while adding his own unique touch. He believes it is this reinterpretation process that keeps storytelling alive and kicking.

Scorsese's personal inspiration was drawn from the annals of Hollywood history through the modern day. His favorite films include John Ford's masterpiece The Searchers, Powell and Pressburger's enthralling creation The Red Shoes, and Federico Fellini’s thought-provoking gem 8½.

Scorsese is also a fan of Orson Welles' iconic magnum opus Citizen Kane, and Luchino Visconti's timeless work, The Leopard. These are among countless other films and shorts that have played a crucial part in shaping Scorsese's creative vision.

It's almost too easy to appreciate Martin Scorsese – the definition of a cinematic genius. His career has been nothing short of extraordinary, and his films are pure gold.

Martin Scorsese's collaborations have wowed generations of film buffs, and left an indelible mark the world of cinema. As we applaud his contributions (and eagerly await what he'll entertain us with next), one thing's for sure: storytelling is in his blood.

Brooks Robinson Biography

Brooks Robinson, born on May 18, 1937, in Little Rock, Arkansas, was a legendary baseball player known for his exceptional skills as a third baseman. He spent his entire career playing for the Baltimore Orioles and made his major league debut in 1955, leaving a lasting impact on the sport.

Throughout his career, Robinson showcased his defensive prowess with numerous accolades and accomplishments. He was a key player in leading the Baltimore Orioles to championships in 1966 and 1970, earning him the World Series MVP title in 1970. His exceptional performance on the field earned him recognition as an 18-time MLB All-Star and the American League MVP in 1964.

Grammy Awards Best Regional Roots Music Album Winner History

The Grammy Award for Best Regional Roots Music Album is presented at the Grammy Awards to recognize quality vocal or instrumental regional forms of American roots music albums. The award was introduced in 2012 and combines several categories such as Best Hawaiian Music Album, Best Zydeco or Cajun Music Album, and Best Native American Music Album. The winner is determined by the National Academy of Recording Arts and Sciences and the current winner is Ranky Tanky – Live at the 2022 New Orleans Jazz & Heritage Festival. Hawaiian musician Kalani Pe’a holds the record for the most wins in this category with three.

Introduction to the Grammy Best Regional Roots Music Album

The Grammy Best Regional Roots Music Album is an award category at the Grammy Awards that celebrates albums in regionally based traditional American music genres. These genres include Hawaiian, Native American, polka, zydeco, and Cajun music. Introduced in 2012 as part of a major category overhaul, this award recognizes artistic achievement and technical proficiency in the recording industry, regardless of album sales or chart position.

This category provides a platform for artists in regional roots music to gain recognition and honor the rich heritage of their respective genres. The Grammy Best Regional Roots Music Album highlights the cultural significance and artistry of these genres, showcasing the diverse musical traditions that contribute to the fabric of American music.

History and Evolution of the Grammy Best Regional Roots Music Album

The Grammy Best Regional Roots Music Album category was introduced in 2012 as part of a major reorganization of Grammy categories, combining several existing categories such as Best Hawaiian Music Album, Best Zydeco or Cajun Music Album, and Best Native American Music Album. This change aimed to provide a more comprehensive recognition of regionally based traditional American music genres, celebrating the rich diversity and cultural contributions of these genres.

In 2021, the category expanded even further to include Go-go music, reflecting the ongoing evolution and inclusivity of the Grammy Awards. This expansion allowed for a broader representation of the vibrant and unique sounds that contribute to the best regional roots music albums.

Throughout its history, the Best Regional Roots Music Album category has played a pivotal role in honoring and preserving traditional American music genres. By recognizing artists and albums in these categories, the Grammy Awards have helped to raise awareness and appreciation for the cultural heritage and artistic excellence within regional roots music.

As the Grammy Awards continue to evolve and adapt to the changing landscape of the music industry, the Best Regional Roots Music Album category remains a testament to the importance of recognizing and honoring the rich history and ongoing impact of regional roots music.
